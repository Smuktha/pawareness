Learn About Phishing

Phishing is a common cyber attack method used by criminals to steal sensitive information. Learn how to recognize phishing attempts and protect yourself.

Phishing Techniques

Email Phishing

Email phishing is one of the most common methods used in cyberattacks. Attackers send fraudulent emails that appear to be from trusted sources to steal sensitive information like login credentials and credit card details.

Website Phishing

Website phishing involves creating a fake website that looks like a legitimate one. Phishers trick users into entering personal details such as usernames, passwords, and payment information.

SMS Phishing (Smishing)

Smishing uses text messages to trick users into clicking malicious links or sharing sensitive information. These attacks often appear to be from legitimate institutions, such as banks or government agencies.

How to Protect Yourself

  • Never click on links or download attachments from unknown or suspicious emails.
  • Always check the sender's email address and look for signs of a fake sender.
  • Look for HTTPS in the website URL to verify it's secure.
  • Use two-factor authentication (2FA) for added security.
  • Keep your software and antivirus programs updated.
